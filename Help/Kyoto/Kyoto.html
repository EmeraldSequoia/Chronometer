Kyoto

Kyoto, the ancient capital of Japan, shows traditional Japanese time like an Edo period clock (和時計 wadokei).

There are two basic ways to make such a clock: with a fixed dial and a hand that moves at different rates for day and night or with a variable dial and a constant rate hand. Kyoto uses the first method on the front and the second on the back. (Some old Japanese clocks used a moving dial and a fixed pointer; Kyoto does not attempt to model that approach.)

Kyoto's stem is on the top and its buttons are on the bottom. The left button advances by a month, the right one by a day (when viewed from the front; they're reversed with the watch is flipped over to the back).
